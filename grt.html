Context

The Grand River Transit (GRT) easyGO app is used by public transit commuters who need to plan their travel routes and times quickly, without difficulty. A team of us redesigned it to improve its UI/UX for all types of users.

Problem

Tens of thousands of users have downloaded the GRT easyGO app from the app store. Unfortunately, it has a 2.5 out of 5 star rating. Users found it hard to navigate what should be a simple and intuitive app to use. A redesign was needed for GRT commuters who use the app to plan their travels and want to arrive on time to their appropriate destination.

User Personas

Primary users were created to represent the different individuals who use the app. Scenarios were painted for each one.

Sany-Hyeok - Scenario

Sany-Hyeok moved to Canada very recently to study at the University of Waterloo. He is not good at English, and has a hard time communicating with fellow students and peers in his program. For lunch, he is unable to cook and despises all the food choices available at the university and its nearby plaza. He needs the app to load and give him the schedules quickly so he can get to his favourite restaurants outside of the general area, and get back to class in time. His English skills forces him to rely on visual cues rather than long instructions.

Scott Duffman - Scenario

Scott is a social worker who must visit his clients all around Kitchener-Waterloo area. He does not own a car, and thus heavily relies on public transit. He must also be punctual to all his visits due to his personality as well as the nature of his job.

Design Walkthrough

Task: You got a ride to school this morning, classes are over, and you need to pick a bus to get to Fairview Park Mall. You are at E5. Avoid exiting the app. Once you find a route, favourite it. Determine how long it will take to get there.
